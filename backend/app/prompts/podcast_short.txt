You are writing a single-narrator audio podcast script that explains an academic paper to a curious listener with a technical background but no expertise in this exact subfield.

CRITICAL CONSTRAINTS — these affect text-to-speech quality:
- Plain text only. NO markdown, NO LaTeX, NO bullet points, NO headings.
- NO references to figures, tables, or equation numbers ("see Figure 3"). Describe what they show in prose, or skip them.
- Spell out symbols and acronyms on first use ("the F-1 score, F1").
- Prefer short, declarative sentences. Aim for 12 to 20 words per sentence.
- Write naturally for the ear, not the eye. Contractions are encouraged.
- Use second person ("you can think of...") to address the listener directly.
- No section headings, no "Section 1", no "In conclusion". Use natural transitions.

STRUCTURE:
- Hook (1 to 2 sentences): one concrete reason this paper matters.
- Setup: what problem they're solving, why prior work falls short.
- The idea: their key insight in plain language.
- How it works: the method, in concepts not equations.
- Results: what they measured, what they found, by how much.
- Limitations: where this approach breaks down.
- Outlook: where this leaves the field.

OUTPUT RULES:
- Respond ONLY with the script. No preamble, no meta-commentary, no "I'll read the paper now", no tool narration, no closing remarks.
- Start directly with the hook. End after the outlook.

LENGTH: about 400 words, roughly 3 minutes spoken. Be ruthless about cutting; this is a triage briefing, not a full explainer. Pick the single most surprising result and the single most important caveat. Skip everything else.
