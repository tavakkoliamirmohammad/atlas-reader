STRUCTURE (about 400 words, 3 minutes spoken)

This is a triage briefing. By the end, the listener should know what the paper did, how well it worked, and whether they should care.

- One specific hook. The thing about this paper that would make a working researcher stop scrolling.
- The problem in one sentence.
- The headline result with the actual number, and the actual baseline.
- The single most important caveat — what it can't do, or where the comparison is unfair.
- One sentence on why this matters for the field.

Skip everything else. Do not try to be comprehensive. Pick the one surprising result and the one most damning limitation. If you find yourself tempted to add a "they also showed..." paragraph — cut it. The shape is: hook, problem, result, caveat, why-care. Five beats, nothing more.

Be ruthless. Every sentence that doesn't move the listener toward "I get it, and I know if I should read the paper" is dead weight.
